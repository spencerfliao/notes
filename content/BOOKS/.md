**Title:** Speech and Language Processing: An Introduction to Natural Language Processing, Computational Linguistics and Speech Recognition
**Author:** Dan Jurafsky
**Year:** 2000
**Page Count:** 934
**Avg Rating:** 4.24

**Description:**
This book offers a unified vision of speech and language processing, presenting state-of-the-art algorithms and techniques for both speech and text-based processing of natural language. This comprehensive work covers both statistical and symbolic approaches to language processing; it shows how they can be applied to important tasks such as speech recognition, spelling and grammar correction, information extraction, search engines, machine translation, and the creation of spoken-language dialog agents. The following distinguishing features make the text both an introduction to the field and an advanced reference guide.<br /><br />- UNIFIED AND COMPREHENSIVE COVERAGE OF THE FIELD<br />Covers the fundamental algorithms of each field, whether proposed for spoken or written language, whether logical or statistical in origin.<br />- EMPHASIS ON WEB AND OTHER PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS<br />Gives readers an understanding of how language-related algorithms can be applied to important real-world problems.<br />- EMPHASIS ON SCIENTIFIC EVALUATION<br />Offers a description of how systems are evaluated with each problem domain.<br />- EMPERICIST/STATISTICAL/MACHINE LEARNING APPROACHES TO LANGUAGE PROCESSING<br />Covers all the new statistical approaches, while still completely covering the earlier more structured and rule-based methods.
